Shiseido

Shiseido Company, Limited is a major Japanese hair care and cosmetics producer. It is one of the oldest cosmetics companies in the world

Shiseido is founded in 1872, and it celebrated its 140th year anniversary in 2012. It is the largest cosmetic firm in Japan and the forth largest cosmetics company in the world.

Arinobu Fukuhara, who former head pharmacist to the Japanese Imperial Navy, established the Shiseido Pharmacy in 1872. After he visit to the United States and Europe, Arinobu added a soda fountain to the store. This later grew into the Shiseido Parlour restaurant business, and led to the introduction of ice cream in Japan. The name "Shiseido" can be translated as "praise the virtues of the earth which nurtures new life and brings forth significant values"

Shiseido was the first to develop the softening lotion, and a lightweight liquid of toner-like consistency. The oldest softening lotion is named Eudermine, which is still marketed and sold at all Shiseido counters.
